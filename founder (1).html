My Thinking Right Now
💡
Why Airbnb first
Cleanest build — fully self-serve, no discovery calls, $199/mo recurring. The Typeform intake replaces every conversation. First client possible within a week of Phase 2 starting. Also building direct insight into the STR market before Starlite Properties activates.
💡
Why Chatbot second
My existing beauty network means I already know my first 10 potential clients. I don't need to find them — just reach out. The $500 setup fee generates immediate cash while the $250/mo retainer compounds. Hair salon chatbot template built once, deployed to 50 salons with minor customisation.
🎯
The one risk I'm watching
Building too many things at once before any generate real revenue. Phase 3 (systematizing) must happen before Phase 4 (scaling). The temptation when first money comes in will be to skip Phase 3 and go straight to building more services. I can't let that happen.
🔮
Most excited about
Trading Journal AI — because I am the customer. I've experienced every problem it solves. When I launch it, I'll be talking to people exactly like me. That authenticity doesn't come from market research. It comes from living it. I also want to build my own trading edge while building the tool.
⚙️
Why the OS matters
The dashboard, phases checklist, and marketing framework aren't just documentation — they're the actual infrastructure. Every new service slots into the same Stripe, Airtable, Make.com. I build the system once. Services plug in. This is what makes 7 services manageable from one screen.
🏗️
The holding company arc
Starlite Group isn't a name for the AI business — it's the actual corporation I'm building under. Ops → Properties → Capital → Ventures. Each division funded by the last. This isn't a sprint, it's a 5-year architecture. The work I do in year one creates the foundation everything else builds on.

The Bigger Picture
Why I'm building this the way I'm building it
Most people who want multiple income streams start by copying what someone else built. I'm doing it differently — designing the holding company structure first, then building the first division to fund the rest. Starlite Ops isn't the destination. It's the engine.

The AI services aren't just a way to make money. Each one teaches me about a market I want to eventually own a piece of — beauty and wellness, short-term rentals, trading, digital marketing. By the time Starlite Properties acquires its first property, I'll have spent a year running an Airbnb automation service. I'll know the economics before I write the first cheque.

The goal isn't to be busy. It's to build systems that generate wealth while I'm doing other things. The business should work harder than I do. By month 9, I want to spend 3 hours a week reviewing a dashboard instead of 40 hours a week doing work. Everything I'm building right now is in service of that.
